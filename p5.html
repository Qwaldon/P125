Plant Care Guide

Vegetables to Grow

Click on any vegetable to learn how to grow it successfully in your backyard

Tomatoes

How to Grow:

Tomatoes are warm-season plants that thrive in full sun (6-8 hours daily). Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before the last frost, or purchase young plants.

  • Plant in well-draining, nutrient-rich soil with pH 6.0-6.8
  • Space plants 24-36 inches apart
  • Provide support with cages or stakes
  • Water deeply 1-2 times per week, keeping foliage dry
  • Fertilize with balanced fertilizer when planting and when fruits form

Harvesting:

Harvest when fruits are fully colored and slightly soft to touch. Pick regularly to encourage more production.

Lettuce

How to Grow:

Lettuce is a cool-season crop that grows quickly and can be harvested multiple times.

  • Plant in early spring or fall when temperatures are between 45-75°F
  • Sow seeds directly in garden 1/4 inch deep, 1 inch apart
  • Thin seedlings to 4-8 inches apart depending on variety
  • Keep soil consistently moist
  • Use shade cloth if temperatures exceed 80°F

Harvesting:

Harvest leaf lettuce by cutting outer leaves when they reach desired size. For head lettuce, wait until heads are firm.

Carrots

How to Grow:

Carrots grow best in loose, sandy soil free from stones that can cause forking.

  • Sow seeds directly in garden 3-4 weeks before last frost
  • Plant seeds 1/4 inch deep, 1/2 inch apart in rows 12-18 inches apart
  • Thin seedlings to 2-3 inches apart when tops are 2 inches tall
  • Keep soil consistently moist
  • Mulch to retain moisture and keep roots cool

Harvesting:

Harvest when roots reach desired size (usually 1/2 to 1 inch in diameter). Loosen soil with a fork before pulling.

Zucchini

How to Grow:

Zucchini is a prolific summer squash that's easy to grow in warm weather.

  • Plant after all danger of frost has passed
  • Sow seeds 1 inch deep in hills (2-3 seeds per hill) spaced 3-4 feet apart
  • Thin to strongest seedling per hill
  • Water deeply once a week, more in extreme heat
  • Fertilize when plants begin to vine

Harvesting:

Harvest when fruits are 6-8 inches long for best flavor. Pick every 1-2 days to encourage production.

Bell Peppers

How to Grow:

Peppers need warm temperatures and a long growing season to produce well.

  • Start seeds indoors 8-10 weeks before last frost
  • Transplant outdoors when soil is warm (at least 65°F)
  • Space plants 18-24 inches apart
  • Provide consistent moisture
  • Use balanced fertilizer when planting and when fruits form

Harvesting:

Harvest when fruits reach full size and color. Use shears to avoid damaging plants.

Plant Care & Monitoring

Essential tips for maintaining a healthy and productive garden

Watering

Most vegetables need 1-2 inches of water per week. Water deeply and less frequently to encourage deep root growth. Water in the morning to reduce evaporation and prevent disease.

Fertilizing

Use organic compost or balanced fertilizer. Heavy feeders like tomatoes and corn need more nutrients than root crops. Follow package instructions and avoid over-fertilizing.

Pest Control

Monitor plants regularly. Use physical barriers, companion planting, or organic pest control methods. Remove diseased plants promptly to prevent spread.

Weed Management

Mulch with straw, leaves, or grass clippings to suppress weeds. Hand-pull weeds when small. Avoid disturbing soil too much to prevent new weed seeds from germinating.

Monitoring

Check plants daily for signs of stress, disease, or pests. Track growth and production in a garden journal. Adjust care based on plant responses.

Support Systems

Provide trellises, cages, or stakes for vining plants like tomatoes, cucumbers, and beans. This improves air circulation and makes harvesting easier.

Seasonal Planting Guide

What to plant when for optimal growth in your backyard garden

Spring

  • Lettuce
  • Spinach
  • Peas
  • Radishes
  • Carrots
  • Broccoli
  • Cabbage

Plant as soon as soil can be worked. Use row covers for frost protection.

Summer

  • Tomatoes
  • Peppers
  • Cucumbers
  • Zucchini
  • Beans
  • Corn
  • Eggplant

Plant after last frost when soil is warm. Provide consistent moisture.

Fall

  • Kale
  • Brussels Sprouts
  • Swiss Chard
  • Beets
  • Turnips
  • Garlic (for next year)
  • Spinach

Plant in late summer for fall harvest. Many crops sweeten after frost.

Winter Planning

  • Plan next year's garden
  • Order seeds
  • Start seeds indoors
  • Improve soil with compost
  • Maintain tools
  • Build new beds
  • Grow microgreens indoors

Use winter to prepare for a successful spring garden.

Gardening Tips & Tricks

Expert advice to help your backyard garden thrive

Soil Preparation

Healthy soil is the foundation of a successful garden. Test your soil and amend with compost annually. Rotate crops to prevent nutrient depletion and disease buildup.

Learn More

Companion Planting

Some plants grow better together. Try planting basil with tomatoes, carrots with onions, or beans with corn. These combinations can deter pests and improve growth.

View Combinations

Container Gardening

Short on space? Many vegetables grow well in containers. Choose compact varieties and ensure pots have good drainage. Tomatoes, peppers, herbs, and lettuce all do well in pots.

Best Container Plants

Extending the Season

Use cold frames, row covers, or greenhouses to grow earlier in spring and later into fall. Some cold-hardy vegetables like kale and spinach can survive winter with protection.

Season Extension Tips

Organic Pest Control

Attract beneficial insects, use floating row covers, hand-pick pests, or try organic sprays like neem oil. Healthy plants are more resistant to pest damage.

Pest Management

Water Conservation

Use drip irrigation or soaker hoses, mulch heavily, water in the morning, and group plants with similar water needs together to reduce water waste.
